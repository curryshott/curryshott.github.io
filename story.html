01 — THE DRIVE

Why I Create

There's a feeling I've chased my whole life — the moment an audience reacts to something you made without knowing why. A sharp inhale. A freeze. A rewatch. That involuntary response is the only metric that matters to me.

Most content gets forgotten in seconds. I've always believed that great media should leave a mark — something felt before it's ever understood. That conviction didn't come from a textbook. It came from paying attention.

Whether it was studying how a cut in a film changes the emotional weight of a scene, or watching a single ad send an entire comment section into a spiral — I became obsessed with the mechanics of feeling. How does media make people feel? Why does some work land and other work disappear?

Archive was built on that obsession. It's not an agency. It's a point of view — executed with the precision of a business and the soul of an artist.

02 — THE PATH

How I Got Here

I started at Indiana University as a finance major — drawn to the logic of markets, the language of value. But I've always known that what I actually wanted to build lived somewhere between business and art. I made the pivot: economics as the foundation, media production as the practice.

That combination changed everything. I wasn't just learning to tell stories — I was learning how stories move, how they distribute value, how they shape perception at scale. At WTIU, Indiana's public television station, I directed a live daily news broadcast. Live. Every day. No room for error, no second takes — just execution under pressure. That experience wired me differently.

03 — THE GAP

Why Archive Exists

I kept seeing the same problem everywhere. On one side: creative agencies with genuine artistic vision and zero understanding of how businesses actually grow. On the other: marketing consultants who knew the numbers cold but couldn't produce content that made anyone feel anything.

The businesses stuck in the middle were paying for one or the other — and getting half the result. No one was sitting at the intersection of craft and commerce. That's the gap Archive was built to fill.

04 — THE PRINCIPLES

What I Believe

01
Feeling Beats Logic
People don't remember what they thought about a piece of content. They remember how it made them feel. The most technically correct strategy in the world loses to a single moment of genuine resonance.
02
Craft Is Non-Negotiable
There's no shortcut to quality. Great work is the result of obsessive attention — to the frame, the pacing, the word choice, the silence. Everything is a decision. Every decision matters.
03
Art and Business Aren't Opposites
The false choice between creativity and commerce is lazy thinking. The best media in the world was made by people who understood both. Archive refuses to choose sides.
